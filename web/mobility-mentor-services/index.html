What We Offer

Three Services. One System.

Standard agencies do a background check. We do placement, training, and retention — because a caregiver who can't afford rent becomes your problem in 60 days.

🤝

Caregiver Placement

We match trained, certified caregivers to disabled adults based on specific needs, lifestyle, daily living requirements, and personal care goals. Pre-screened. Peer-mentorship trained. Built for independent living, not just personal care tasks.

Core Service
🏠

Housing Stability Programme

Housing insecurity drives 80% of caregiver turnover. Our housing stability support removes the financial instability that forces caregivers out of the direct care workforce — protecting both the caregiver and the client relationship.

Retention Model
📋

Training & Certification

Peer-mentorship-based training in independent living support, personal care, and disability-inclusive practices. Produces better retention outcomes than standard agency training. Completion earns a caregiver certification that means more than a background check.

Differentiation

How It Works

From Inquiry to Stable Care in Four Steps

A placement process designed around long-term continuity, not just the first handoff.

1

Needs Assessment

We map your specific daily living requirements, personal care needs, lifestyle, and support goals before matching begins.

2

Caregiver Matching

We match from our pool of trained, certified workers — screened for fit, not just availability. Independent living focus is non-negotiable.

3

Training & Onboarding

Your caregiver completes our peer-mentorship programme if not already certified. Disability-inclusive practices. Personal care. Community participation.

4

Housing Support Activated

We stabilise the caregiver's housing situation — removing the #1 reason caregivers leave within 90 days, protecting your care continuity.

The Four Pillars

What Your Partnership Funds

Each pillar is a named, fundable initiative with measurable outcomes — designed for foundation reporting, ESG alignment, and named giving opportunities.

Pillar 01

Training & Certification

Entry-level gift or grant supporting caregiver training and certification. Peer-mentorship model produces better retention than standard agency training. Measurable output: caregivers trained and placed. Named giving opportunity available.

Pillar 02

Talent Pipeline

Mid-tier partnership supporting recruitment, matching, and career pathway development for direct care workers. ESG and DEI reporting eligible. Addresses the structural supply shortage driving the care gap.

Pillar 03

Compensation Advocacy

Advocacy and policy-level investment supporting caregiver compensation reform. Addresses the $16.82/hr median wage crisis driving 80% turnover. Corporate CSR alignment opportunity. Bipartisan, underserved space.

Pillar 04

Housing Stability

Premier-level foundation or corporate partnership. Housing insecurity is the primary driver of caregiver dropout. Named initiative opportunity. Protects client care continuity by stabilising the caregiver workforce at its root.
